# Deliverable 3 — Risk and Uncertainty Register

This register separates **what is known, what is contested, and what is unknown**,
as required by the brief. It uses a structure compatible with the UK Government
Analysis Function approach to communicating uncertainty: each item records the
**state of knowledge**, the **direction of risk** (does getting it wrong push us
toward over- or under-regulation?), the **likelihood and impact**, and the
**evidence**.

## Scoring key

- **Likelihood:** Low / Medium / High (probability the risk materialises within the next ~10 years).
- **Impact:** Low / Medium / High (severity for public safety, environment, or policy credibility if it does).
- **Knowledge state:** KNOWN (well evidenced) / CONTESTED (experts disagree) / UNKNOWN (data gap).

## The "known / contested / unknown" summary the brief asks for

**KNOWN (act on confidently):**

- Fusion has no chain-reaction, meltdown, or long-lived high-level-waste hazard [iterstafetybasis], [ukfusionregfactsheet].
- Tritium and neutron-activated materials are the genuine radiological hazards [iterstafetybasis].
- Both the US and UK have committed to proportionate, fission-distinct regimes [nrc2023vote], [ukfusionregfactsheet].
- A too-heavy regime would impose real economic and competitiveness costs [crs2026fusion].

**CONTESTED (design for adaptability):**

- Whether the chosen light regimes adequately bound tritium discharge and activated-waste handling [iaeatritiumharmonization], [radwasterecycling2023].
- Whether fusion waste should default to disposal or shift to recycling/clearance [radwasterecycling2023].
- How to build adequate safety cases with limited operating experience [sidmethodology2026].

**UNKNOWN (resolve through monitoring and staged review):**

- Real commercial-scale activation inventories and waste volumes [tritiumbreeding2018].
- The date and dominant technology of first commercial grid connection [crs2026fusion].
- Long-run performance of closed D-T fuel cycles at scale [iaeatecdoc2049].

## How the register shapes the recommendation

The highest-impact risks (R1 over-regulation, R2 under-regulation, S1 trust) pull
in opposite directions, and several key parameters are **unknown** rather than
merely contested. This combination — high stakes plus genuine data gaps — argues
against any fixed, one-shot regime and in favour of a **proportionate but
adaptive, review-gated framework** (Option C in Deliverable 4).
